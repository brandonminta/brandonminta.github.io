About Me

My academic journey has equipped me with a strong foundation in computational modeling, data science, and high-performance computing. I enjoy tackling complex problems using innovative technologies, with the aim of contributing to both academic research and industry advancements.

Professional Development

I have taken part in various schools and workshops that have allowed me to expand my knowledge and collaborate with experts from different fields.

Minicourse on Lattice Models and Applications to Biological Problems

ICTP-SAIFR, Brazil

The course focused on using lattice models to study biological and ecological systems, including population dynamics. It combined strong theoretical modeling with computational implementation, providing a comprehensive understanding of how to model and simulate complex biological processes.

Complex Systems School at Yachay

Yachay Tech University, Ecuador

As a member of the Complex Systems Research Group, I collaborated in organizing and delivering the First Complex Systems School in Ecuador, which brought together leading international researchers. The program covered a range of topics in biological and geological systems, machine learning, and social simulations, with practical applications of network analysis in crime and soccer. Supported by ICTP funding, this free course created a platform for innovative discussions and collaborations in complex systems research.
